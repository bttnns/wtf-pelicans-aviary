    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">why nest at home</h2>
    <ul class="ml-5 list-disc space-y-1">
      <li>
        <b>Privacy.</b> The bird never leaves the nest. Your prompts stay on
        your device. The lab cannot see what you asked. This matters more than
        people admit.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Free.</b> No tokens, no meter, no "you have used 80% of your
        allocation" email at 2am. Generate a thousand pelicans at 3am for the
        cost of electricity.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Yours.</b> No rate limits, no terms of service deciding what your
        bird may draw. No model quietly "updated" to be less weird overnight.
        Your bird stays exactly as unhinged as the day you adopted it.
      </li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">the two-files truth</h2>
    <p>
      Back in the{" "}
      <a href="/pelicanplus/parameter" class="text-blue-700 underline">parameters</a>{" "}
      lesson: a model is concretely <b>just two files</b>, a big parameters file
      and a small run file. That abstraction has teeth here. Llama 2 70B is 140
      GB of parameters and roughly 500 lines of C, nothing else. Put both on a
      laptop, compile, talk to the model, no internet. The whole frontier
      compressed into a backpack. Your backpack just needs to be sturdy: newer
      birds are bigger. But a model is a file, and files can be owned and run in
      a garage.
    </p>
    <p class="mt-3">
      Sit with that for a second: the descendant of the most expensive research
      program our species has ever run now nests on your laptop, off the grid,
      owned outright. You are running history on a graphics card.
    </p>

    <p>
      Cross-platform (Mac, Linux, Windows). Install from{" "}
      <a href="https://ollama.com" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer" class="text-blue-700 underline">ollama.com</a>,
      then in a terminal:

    <pre class="mt-2 overflow-x-auto whitespace-pre-wrap break-words rounded bg-black p-3 text-xs text-lime-400">{ollamaSnippet}</pre>
    <p class="mt-2">
      Ollama manages downloads, quantization, and a local API server. Pull a
      bird by name, talk to it. The community library covers most of the
      current open-weight flock.
    </p>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">the friendly way: LM Studio</h2>
    <p>
      <b>LM Studio</b> (
      <a href="https://lmstudio.ai" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer" class="text-blue-700 underline">lmstudio.ai</a>)
      is the no-terminal nest: a desktop app for Mac, Windows, and Linux. Browse
      a catalog, click download, start chatting. It tells you which models fit
      your memory before you commit, runs MLX-format birds natively on Apple
      Silicon, and can serve a local API if you outgrow the GUI. If you have
      never run a model at home before, start here.
    </p>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">what your nest needs</h2>
    <ul class="ml-5 list-disc space-y-1">
      <li>
        <b>Mac (Apple Silicon).</b> The best perch for the money. Unified memory
        means GPU and CPU share the same pool: a 32 GB M-series Mac comfortably
        runs a 30B bird at full speed. 64 GB opens the bigger flocks. M4 Max at
        128 GB runs Llama 4 Scout (109B MoE) without breaking a sweat.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Linux or Windows with a GPU.</b> It is all about VRAM. About 6 GB
        runs a quantized 8B bird. 12 to 16 GB gets you 14 to 32B. 24 GB opens
        the serious flock. MoE birds like Qwen3.6-35B-A3B (35B total, 3B active
        per token) let a 12 GB card run a 35B-class model at a respectable clip.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Just a CPU?</b> It still works. The bird paddles slower. Start with
        a 3 to 8B model.
      </li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">good open-weight birds to adopt in 2026</h2>
    <p>
      The flock that costs nothing and asks for nothing has never been stronger:
    </p>
    <ul class="ml-5 mt-2 list-disc space-y-1">
      <li>
        <b>Llama 4 Scout</b> (Meta): 109B total, 17B active (MoE). 10-million-token
        context. Natively multimodal. Fits on a single H100 or a 128 GB Mac.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Qwen3 / Qwen3.5 / Qwen3.6</b> (Alibaba, Apache 2.0): spans from a
        0.6B edge bird to a 235B MoE flagship. The{" "}
        <b>Qwen3.6-35B-A3B</b> variant (35B total, 3B active, 262K context) is
        the best practical local bird for most tasks in mid-2026.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>DeepSeek V4 Pro</b> (MIT): 1.6T total parameters, 49B active,
        1-million-token context. Wins agentic coding benchmarks. Needs serious
        hardware, but the weights are yours free and clear.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Gemma 4</b> (Google, Apache 2.0): the 26B MoE variant activates only
        4B per token, 256K context, consumer hardware. Strong reasoning for
        its size.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Phi-4</b> (Microsoft, MIT): 14B parameters, punches well above its
        weight on reasoning. Runs at 40 to 60 tok/s on an M3/M4 Mac.
      </li>
      <li>
        <b>Mistral Medium 3.5</b> (Mistral, Modified MIT): 128B dense model,
        reasoning plus vision plus coding in one download.
      </li>
    </ul>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">the loco birds draw the wildest pelicans</h2>
    <p>
      <i>Local</i> is short for <i>loco</i>. The polished frontier models draw
      a suspiciously competent pelican. Your backyard bird hands you a
      six-legged, two-headed creature with handlebars fused through its beak,
      pedaling a bicycle that is also somehow a fish. That chaos is the good
      stuff. Treasure it.
    </p>

    <h2 class="wtf-title mt-7 mb-2 text-2xl text-purple-900">
      frontier flock vs. your backyard bird (the scale)
    </h2>
    <p>
      The frontier flock is raised on tens of thousands of GPUs. The biggest
      closed models do not publish parameter counts, but the working assumption
      in 2026 is hundreds of billions active per token, trained on 15 to 30
      trillion tokens. Your backyard bird is 3 to 70 billion and fits on a
      laptop.
    </p>
    <p class="mt-3">
      But watch how fast that gap closes. Open-weight models stopped chasing raw
      parameter counts and started winning on benchmarks. DeepSeek V4 Pro ties
      the closed frontier on agentic coding. Kimi K2.6 (1T MoE, Modified MIT)
      sits at number 4 in the global intelligence index. A 7 to 8 billion bird
      you can run at home today clears the bar GPT-3.5 (175B) set in 2022, and
      open-weight 30 to 70 billion birds now out-draw the original GPT-4 and
      Claude 3 Opus from two years ago.
    </p>
    <p class="mt-3">
      <b>Today&apos;s backyard bird is last year&apos;s frontier model, minus
      the warehouse.</b> Every year the gap closes another notch, and the bird
      on your laptop gets a little less loco. A little. Not entirely.
    </p>
